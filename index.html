Optimizing Broiler Growth Curves via First-Order Derivatives

In closed-house Malaysian poultry systems (such as those in Perak and Johor), broilers face hot, humid climates. Tracking total weight $f(x)$ is insufficient; standard operation procedures (SOPs) require animal technologists to evaluate the instantaneous growth rate $f'(x)$ (grams/day). This tells us exactly when feed conversion is optimal, when heat-stress mitigation systems (evaporative cooling pads) must be maximized, and when metabolic load peaks.

Mathematical Modeling of Broiler Growth Curve

Connecting biological growth dynamics directly to calculus parameters

The Logistic Growth Function: $f(x)$

Biomedical growth is characterized by an initial lag phase, an exponential acceleration phase, an inflection point, and a deceleration plateau. The most accurate function used by agricultural bio-statisticians to model this is the Sigmoid Logistic Growth Model:

Growth Function

f(x) = L
1 + e-k(x - x₀)

  • x: Age of the broiler flock in days (from 0 to 42 days).
  • L: The asymptotic maximum limit of live body weight in grams.
  • k: Logistic growth rate coefficient, representing feed quality & ambient environmental parameters.
  • x₀: The day at which maximum acceleration transitions into deceleration (the curve's inflection point).

The First Derivative: $f'(x)$

Using standard calculus rules (specifically the Quotient Rule or Chain Rule for composite functions), we compute the derivative of $f(x)$ with respect to time ($x$). The resulting rate represents the daily weight gain (grams per day):

Derivative Function

f'(x) = L · k · e-k(x - x₀)
( 1 + e-k(x - x₀) )²

At any precise moment $x$, $f'(x)$ outputs the speed of muscle-tissue development. In Animal Husbandry Science, identifying the local maximum of $f'(x)$ (which analytically occurs exactly at $x = x_0$ with peak speed = $L \cdot k / 4$) is critical to calculating optimal Feed Conversion Efficiency.

DVS Malaysia Regulations

The Department of Veterinary Services Malaysia mandates the Code of Veterinary Practice for Broiler Poultry Farming. Standard SOPs require checking relative and daily incremental weights weekly to detect viral/bacterial outbreak indexes early.

Source Standard dvs.gov.my

Heat Stress & Ventilation

Under standard Malaysian conditions, relative humidity regularly hovers at 80%. When daily growth rates ($f'(x)$) surpass 100g/day, the metabolic heat production of broilers rises exponentially, requiring tunnel ventilation velocities of up to 2.5m/s.
